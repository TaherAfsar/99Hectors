1. Loan eligibility?

Home loan eligibility depends upon the repayment capacity, income, existing loans or debts and age of the loan applicant. The lending company or banks provide online services such as home loan eligibility calculator to calculate loan eligibility of the home loan borrower. The maximum loan that can be sanctioned varies with the banks and the eligibility criteria may vary according to the bank or RBI regulations. As home loan rates increase, the loan eligibility for a borrower becomes stiffer.

2. How Much Minimum Down Payment is Required for Home Loan?

The fact that home loans have made life easy for millions of people in India is no understatement. It is a very affordable solution for anyone dreaming of owning a house or investing in a real estate property. Why? Because a home loan lets you pay the property cost in easy-to-manage EMIs. But do you get the entire property cost as a loan from your lender? The answer is no. This is where a home loan down payment comes in. It is the upfront cost that you as a home buyer pay for the purchase of the property, with the remaining property cost to be paid as home loan EMIs. In this blog, we will talk about what a down payment is, what is the minimum down payment for a home loan, tips to manage down payment and more.

3. What is the Minimum Down Payment Required for Home Loan?

As per RBI/NHB guidelines, banks and financial institutions can offer up to 90% LTV (loan to value), depending upon the property cost.

4. What is Home Loan Down Payment?


Before sanctioning a home loan, your lender will thoroughly examine your home loan application and the pertaining documents. The factors that a lender looks at are:

  • Age of the applicant
  • Income of the applicant
  • The credit score of the applicant
  • Existing loans of the applicant
  • Occupation/nature of business of the applicant
  • Income tax returns of the applicant
  • Property value

Depending on these factors, the lender will be able to tell you the maximum amount of housing loan you are eligible for. Ultimately, you will not get 100% property value in the form of a home loan. There is a certain percentage of the property cost that you have to pay upfront to the seller. This is called the down payment on house or ‘one’s own contribution’ to the property purchase.

5. Benefits of Paying a Bigger Down Payment for Home Loan

If you are able, then we recommend paying as much down payment on the house as financially possible. Of course, it should not come at the expense of your savings or risking your financial security. There are many benefits of doing so:

  • our home loan amount will be reduced which ultimately reduces your overall liability.
  • With a lower home loan amount, you will most likely get more favourable interest rates and lower EMI from your lender.
  • You can pay off your home loan much quicker.
